About Us

Why Choose Us

  • Protein: Your dog should be fed high-quality protein that comes mainly from meat. Poultry, beef, lamb, fish or eggs are excellent sources of protein. Avoid foods in which the main source of protein is plant-based ingredients.
  • Fats: Fats are an important source of energy for your dog. Choose healthy fats, such as those contained in fish, vegetable oils (such as flaxseed oil or salmon oil), and fats derived from meat.
  • Carbohydrates: Carbohydrates are a source of energy, but should not be the main component of your dog's diet. It is recommended to choose complex carbohydrates, such as whole-grain cereal products (such as brown rice) and vegetables.
  • Vegetables and fruits: Adding vegetables and fruits to your dog's diet can provide additional vitamins , minerals and fiber. Some dogs enjoy eating raw vegetables, such as carrots and spinach, but consider boiling or steaming vegetables to make them easier to digest.
  • Supplements: Some dogs may need additional supplements, such as glucosamine and chondroitin for healthy joints, or omega-3 fatty acids for healthy skin and coat. However, always consult your veterinarian before adding supplements to your dog's diet.
  • Avoid artificial additives: Use foods that do not contain artificial colors, preservatives or other artificial additives.
  • Regularity and portion control: It is important to feed your dog regularly at consistent times and control portion sizes to avoid being overweight or underweight.
